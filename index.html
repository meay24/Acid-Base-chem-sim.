What the model does

Particles bounce in the beaker. When acid and base collide, they neutralize and are replaced by product particles. Strong electrolytes react readily on collision; weak acids and bases react more slowly, reflecting their partial dissociation in water.

Reaction Chemistry

Net ionic equation: H⁺ + OH⁻ → H₂O

Both HCl and NaOH are strong electrolytes that fully dissociate in water. The equivalence point occurs at pH 7.00 because neither Na⁺ nor Cl⁻ hydrolyzes water.

pH Calculation Method

Strong + Strong: pH = −log[H⁺] or 14 + log[OH⁻] from excess concentration. At equivalence, [H⁺] = [OH⁻] = 10⁻⁷, so pH = 7.00.
